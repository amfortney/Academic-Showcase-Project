# Academic-Showcase-Project
For our Academic showcase, we plan to use the algorithms Breadth-first and Depth-first. These algorithms are best used to find the shortest or most efficient paths between points. Due to this, we have chosen to apply this to our own campus here at Siena University. Our plan is to take our own schedules for this semester and wherever we would start on campus, be it from dorms, townhouses, other buildings, or the entrance, and determine the shortest routes to our classes. The cost of each path will be determined by the distance in either yards or meters.
We believe this study is important because it helps with planning time to travel to classes, so it will help avoid being too late or too early to class.
We will use METAL data by using the map of Siena as our main way of coordinating and determining distance.
